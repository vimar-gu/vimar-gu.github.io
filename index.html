About Me

I'm currently a Ph.D. student at College of Control Science and Engineering, Zhejiang University, Hangzhou, China. I'm supervised by Prof. Wei Jiang. I received my Bachelor's degree in Zhejiang University in 2019. My current research interests include unsupervised domain adaptation, image retrieval and person re-identification.

Projects

NAIC 2020 AI Challenge --- Person Re-identification Track Second Prize

Introduced channel shuffle operations for multi-modal scenario. Further improved the robustness and performance of the model with unsupervised training methods.

Visual Domain Adaptation Challenge 2020 in ECCV Workshop --- Champion

Solved the inter-domain bias and the intra-domain bias brought by camera differences with generative networks, clustering-based training pipeline and post-processing methods.

Angular Triplet Loss for Vehicle Re-identification

Uniformed the metric space for triplet loss and cross entropy loss. Designed an effective baseline with only global feature employed.

Robocup 2018 Montreal --- Small Size League Champion

Robocup is a robot soccer match. In small size league, two groups of robots compete to score goals just like humans. I was in charge of robot motion control in the team.
